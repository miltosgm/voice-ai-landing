Hospitality Sector Overview

Cyprus welcomes over 3.5 million tourists annually, generating roughly €2.7 billion in tourism revenue. Hotels, resorts, boutique properties, and restaurants are the backbone of the Cypriot economy and increasingly technology-dependent.

A hotel without reliable WiFi loses bookings. A restaurant with a slow POS during peak hours loses revenue and reputation. A resort hit by a cyberattack faces regulatory fines and permanent reputational damage.

Key Pain Points & CDMA Solutions

Pain Point 1

Unreliable WiFi — The #1 Guest Complaint

Poor signal, dead zones, and network crashes during peak hours directly impact TripAdvisor reviews and repeat bookings.

CDMA's Solution:

  • Full site WiFi survey and coverage mapping before deployment
  • Enterprise-grade APs (Ubiquiti, Cisco Meraki) with centralised management
  • Network segmentation: separate VLANs for guests, staff, POS, management
  • Dual ISP failover — traffic switches automatically if one line drops
  • Branded guest WiFi portal with usage limits and analytics
  • Remote monitoring with proactive alerts before issues become outages

💡 ROI: One bad TripAdvisor review citing WiFi can cost 3–5 future bookings. CDMA's WiFi solution costs less than that lost revenue.

Pain Point 2

POS Downtime During Peak Service

Restaurants and hotel F&B outlets lose thousands per hour when POS fails on a busy Saturday night or peak tourist week.

CDMA's Solution:

  • POS infrastructure audit — hardware, connectivity, power, integration
  • Reliable network backbone ensuring POS always has connectivity
  • UPS installation on critical POS hardware
  • Extended helpdesk hours during peak season (April–October)
  • On-site SLA with guaranteed response times

💡 ROI: A 2-hour POS outage during a busy dinner service = €2,000–€5,000 in lost revenue. One CDMA contract pays for itself the first time it prevents that.

Pain Point 3

Cybersecurity Exposure & GDPR Liability

Hotels collect passport scans, credit card details, email addresses. Prime targets for cyberattacks with heavy GDPR fines if breached.

CDMA's Solution:

  • Network firewall installation and configuration (Fortinet, SonicWall)
  • Endpoint detection and response (EDR) on all staff devices
  • PCI-DSS readiness review for card-handling businesses
  • GDPR compliance support — data mapping, privacy policy, breach response
  • Staff cybersecurity awareness training (annual or bi-annual)
  • Dark web monitoring for compromised credentials

💡 ROI: Average cost of an SME data breach in Europe: €200,000+. GDPR fines up to 4% of annual turnover. CDMA's security stack is a fraction of that.

Pain Point 4

No In-House IT = No One to Call

Most hospitality businesses call their nephew or restart the router. This reactive approach is costly and stressful.

CDMA's Solution:

  • Fully Managed IT — CDMA acts as the client's IT department
  • Remote Monitoring & Management (RMM) — issues detected before client notices
  • IT asset inventory and documentation maintained by CDMA
  • Patch management — systems kept up-to-date automatically
  • Dedicated account manager — one point of contact for everything
Pain Point 5

Seasonal Peaks Create IT Stress

Cyprus hospitality runs at 30% in winter, 95%+ in summer. Infrastructure must handle that swing.

CDMA's Solution:

  • Capacity planning as part of annual IT review
  • Scalable cloud infrastructure for seasonal demand spikes
  • Rapid provisioning for seasonal staff accounts and devices
  • Temporary site connectivity for pop-up venues, events, weddings
  • Pre-season IT health check before peak season starts

Sales Talking Points & Objection Handling

"Most hotels and restaurants in Cyprus don't have an IT department. They have a person who restarts the router. CDMA changes that — we become your IT department. Local, fast, proactive, and accountable."

The 3-Minute Pitch

  1. Hook: "How much did the last IT problem cost you? Downtime, lost bookings, a bad TripAdvisor review?"
  2. Problem: "Most hospitality businesses in Cyprus are one WiFi crash away from a guest relations nightmare — with no one on call to fix it fast."
  3. Solution: "CDMA is a Cyprus-based managed IT provider that specialises in hospitality. We monitor your systems 24/7, respond fast, and prevent most issues before you notice them."
  4. Proof: Reference a relevant client type and problem solved.
  5. CTA: "Let us do a free IT audit of your property — no cost, no obligation. You'll walk away knowing exactly where your risks are."

Objection Handling

❓ "We already have an IT guy."
We work alongside in-house IT all the time. We cover the gaps: 24/7 monitoring, after-hours support, specialised cybersecurity, and extra capacity during peak season. Your IT person gets better tools and backup — not replaced.
❓ "It's too expensive."
What did your last IT emergency cost you? Emergency call-out fees, lost revenue from downtime, staff hours firefighting — it adds up. Our flat monthly fee is typically cheaper than two or three emergency call-outs per year.
❓ "We manage fine as we are."
Most businesses say that until the summer season when everything spikes at once. Our offer is a free IT audit — let us look under the hood. If everything is genuinely fine, we'll tell you. No hard sell.
❓ "We use [specific IT provider] already."
Do they specialise in hospitality? Do they know guest/staff network segmentation for PCI-DSS? Do they understand the seasonal pressure your systems face? That's our focus — hospitality IT, not generic IT support.
❓ "We don't have budget right now."
When does your budget cycle reset? Would it be worth scheduling a free audit now so you have the full picture when you're ready to decide? No commitment needed.
❓ "We had a bad experience with an IT company before."
That's more common than you'd think — many generic IT providers don't understand hospitality's pace and pressure. What went wrong? I'd like to understand what "good" looks like for you, and whether we're genuinely the right fit.

Onboarding Process

CDMA's onboarding is fast, low-disruption, and confidence-building. Goal: client fully under management within 10–15 business days, feeling value within the first 30 days.

1
Phase 1 — Discovery & Audit (Days 1–3)
Kick-off call · Site visit & physical walkthrough · IT asset inventory · Network diagram · Security posture assessment · WiFi coverage check · Vendor contract review · Credentials documentation
Deliverable: IT Audit Report with findings, risk rating, and recommended actions
2
Phase 2 — Baseline & Onboarding Setup (Days 4–7)
Deploy RMM agents · Configure network monitoring · Set up alerting rules · Install endpoint protection · Configure backups · Create helpdesk account · Brief on-site staff
Deliverable: All systems live in CDMA dashboard; client receives portal login
3
Phase 3 — Quick Wins & Remediation (Days 8–12)
Address critical vulnerabilities · Implement network segmentation · Upgrade EOL hardware · Configure guest WiFi portal · Establish ISP failover · POS connectivity confirmation · UPS installation
Deliverable: Remediation complete; updated risk register shared with client
4
Phase 4 — Handover & Go-Live (Days 13–15)
Final walkthrough · Handover of network diagram & asset register · Confirm escalation matrix · SLA document signed · Account manager introduction · Staff briefing · 30-day check-in scheduled
Deliverable: Signed SLA, full documentation package, client goes live
5
Phase 5 — 30-Day Review
Review first 30 days · Client satisfaction check-in · Address outstanding remediation · Plan pre-season health check · Upsell conversation if natural
Deliverable: 30-day report; updated 6-month roadmap
